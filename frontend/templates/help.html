Help & Instructions

About RIQ Lab Matcher

Welcome to RIQ Lab Matcher! This platform helps you find and connect with research opportunities at top institutions across the United States.

Current Coverage

Our database includes researchers and professors from 8 leading institutions:

  • MIT — Computer Science, Biology, Engineering, and more
  • Harvard University — Medical School, Engineering, Sciences, and more
  • Stanford University — Engineering, Sciences, Medicine, and more
  • Yale University — Sciences, Engineering, Medicine, and more
  • Princeton University — Engineering, Sciences, and more
  • Boston University — Engineering, Sciences, Medicine, and more
  • Northeastern University — Engineering, Sciences, and more
  • Tufts University — Engineering, Sciences, Medicine, and more

Data Sources: Profiles are sourced from institutional websites, public directories, and OpenAlex, updated regularly.

Getting Started

Step 1: Create an Account

Click on "Sign Up" in the navigation bar to create your account. You'll need:

  • A valid email address
  • A secure password

Step 2: Complete Your Profile

After signing up, you'll be asked to answer 5 quick questions about your research interests:

  • Research Field — your broad area of interest (e.g., Biology, Computer Science)
  • Specific Topics — the research topics you're most interested in
  • Academic Level — undergrad, masters, PhD, or postdoc
  • Work Style — experimental (wet lab), computational (dry lab), or both
  • Funding Needs — whether you need a funded position

This profile powers your personalized lab matches on the My Matches page.

Step 3: Browse Labs

Navigate to the Browse Labs page to explore all available PIs and their research labs.

  • Use Department to filter by broad category (e.g., Biology, Engineering), then optionally narrow down by Subfield
  • Use Institution to filter by school
  • Use Location to find labs near you — just start typing to search
  • Use the search bar to search by name, research area, or institution across all PIs

Step 4: Save PIs

When you find labs that interest you:

  • Click the "Save" button on any PI (you must be logged in)
  • View all your saved PIs on the Saved PIs page
  • Compare multiple labs side-by-side using the compare feature
  • Export a report of your saved PIs for reference

Step 5: Send Emails

Send professional cold emails to PIs:

  • Click "Email" on any PI card, or go to Send Email
  • Fill in your name, email, background, and research interests
  • The tool generates a professional email draft that opens in your email client

Tips for Success

  • Research First: Read about each PI's research areas before reaching out
  • Be Specific: Mention specific research projects or papers that interest you
  • Show Enthusiasm: Express genuine interest in their work
  • Be Professional: Use the email draft tool, but personalize it further
  • Follow Up: If you don't hear back, send a polite follow-up after 1-2 weeks

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to attend one of the listed schools?

No! RIQ is open to all students and researchers seeking research opportunities, regardless of your home institution.

How many schools does RIQ cover?

We currently feature researchers from 8 top institutions: MIT, Harvard, Stanford, Yale, Princeton, Boston University, Northeastern, and Tufts. We're continuously expanding our coverage.

How do I know if a lab is accepting students?

Contact the PI directly using the email provided. Many labs welcome inquiries from motivated students.

Can I save multiple PIs?

Yes! Save as many PIs as you'd like. There's no limit to your saved list.

How does the matching algorithm work?

After completing your 5-question profile, our matching algorithm scores each PI based on topic alignment, research evidence, and practical factors like email availability and funding. Results are ranked from best fit to least fit.

How accurate is the email draft tool?

The tool creates professional email drafts, but you should always review and personalize them before sending.

Need More Help?

If you have additional questions or encounter any issues, please contact the RIQ Lab Matcher support team.
